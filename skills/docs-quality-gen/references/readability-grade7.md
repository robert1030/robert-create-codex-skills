# Grade-7 Readability

## Purpose

Use these rules when a document is for handoff, operation, or review by readers who need a clear main line before the technical details.

Grade-7 readability means the reader can understand the main path without guessing. It does not mean removing technical accuracy.

## Core Rules

- Say the plain-language idea first, then give the technical term.
- Keep one main idea in each sentence when possible.
- Split long paragraphs into smaller paragraphs, lists, or labeled blocks.
- Explain a technical term the first time it appears.
- Use labels such as `Problem`, `Reason`, `What to do`, `How to verify`, and `If different` when they make a section easier to follow.
- Avoid relying only on English abbreviations.
- Explain what an example is for. Do not only paste a command.
- Add plain-language warnings for high-risk or easy-to-misread steps.
- Do not delete important limits just to make the text easier.

## Not Required

- Do not remove all English.
- Do not replace every technical term with casual speech.
- Do not make the document read like a children's book.
- Do not sacrifice technical correctness.
